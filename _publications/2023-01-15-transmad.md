abstract: |
  Adversarial patch attacks can fool deep networks with localized, physically realizable perturbations. We present **TransMAD**, a transformer-based masked adversarial defense that models spatial context and suppresses adversarial signal while preserving semantics on benign inputs. Our design plugs into standard vision backbones with modest overhead. Experiments on image classification benchmarks show reduced vulnerability to patch attacks compared with strong baselines, while maintaining competitive clean accuracy—highlighting the role of attention and masking for robust localized defenses.
