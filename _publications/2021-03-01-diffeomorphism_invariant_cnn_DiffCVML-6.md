abstract: |
  Convolutional neural networks are not inherently stable under smooth deformations of the input, which can matter for geometric and medical imaging. We study convolutional architectures whose outputs are approximately invariant to diffeomorphisms of the domain. Building on the structure of convolution and Jacobian-based analysis, we propose building blocks that encourage stability under such deformations. We discuss connections to equivariant and geometry-aware design and evaluate behavior on synthetic and image-based tasks where diffeomorphic deformations are relevant.
