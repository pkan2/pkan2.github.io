abstract: |
  Social media during disasters can reveal how public attention and concerns evolve in real time. We apply a **guided** latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA) approach to Twitter data collected during Hurricane Laura, incorporating domain-specific guidance to improve topic coherence and interpretability. The method helps track evolving latent themes—such as evacuation, safety, and infrastructure—over the course of the event. We discuss how guided topic models can support situational awareness and complement traditional crisis informatics pipelines, and we report qualitative and quantitative observations on topic dynamics during the hurricane period.
